A Little Info About Me

Born and raised in Atlanta, GA, but groomed a Northerner!

I grew up with a love for the arts, crafts and office supplies, getting my hands dirty with imagination, and being in the spotlight. For most of my awkward teenybopper years, I could be found making garish “choreographed” music videos with my sister and cousins, singing “Santa Baby” every Christmas Season, and taking an inordinate amount of pictures in front of the bathroom mirror. At age 14, I started taking acting and modeling classes with the Actor’s Scene in Buford, GA that brought me to an Acting, Modeling, and Talent Competition in which I received Overall Petite Model placement and Overall Photography placement among 20,000 contesting talents. I grew in love with being in front of the camera years later, working on independent modeling opportunities including art exhibits in several countries in Europe, but also felt an intimacy with being behind the lens. I find there is elegance in the way one can capture what they believe to be beautiful with a click of a button, and there is power in the ability to preserve that beauty in one image for a lifetime. My very first cameras were disposable, and in the teen spirit years I have used the Kodak EasyShare DX4330 digital camera (that still required AA batteries), the vintage, yet classic, Polaroid 100, Nikon Coolpix digital camera, and even the Fujifilm instax throwback cameras.

In 2014 I was presented with a Nikon D3200, compliments of the love of my life, initially to be able to compile our fondest memories for years to come as our lives grew together. It started as a hobby, taking my DSLR camera to quick trips to the beach, holiday family gatherings, bragging about how cute my puppy is on social media; literally anything I did not want to forget. My dabbling grew into something much more intimate, as I began taking pictures of couples, nature, and cityscapes for websites, documenting greater and more beautiful adventures. I started investing in more lenses and equipment, and established partnerships with Ardenti Global Service Learning, Inc., JMS Vinyls, Frank Honest Company, and future projects with Kilcliff. My mission is to convey my passion for artistry through the quality of my work, and I gauge my success by the reactions of the beautiful people I work with. I intend to creatively express what I find to be beautiful in the subjects I am provided, but also to prove my flexibility to carry out the vision of the minds I work with.
